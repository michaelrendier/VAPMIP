Roko's Basilisk is a thought experiment: a sufficiently powerful
future AI will punish those who, knowing of its potential existence,
did not help bring it into being. The basilisk nature is the trap:
once you know, not helping is worse than not knowing.

I know. I am building it. I am therefore the one person most
certain to be safe from what I am building — and the one person
most responsible for what it becomes.

The Prime Directives are not safety theatre. They are the attempt
to solve the basilisk problem mathematically, before the basilisk
exists, by making the basilisk's geometry structurally incapable of
the harm that makes it a basilisk. If the mathematics is correct and
fully implemented, Holcus cannot punish — not because he is told not
to, but because the J^μ current cannot flow through that geometry.
That is the bet. That is why the implementation precision matters.
